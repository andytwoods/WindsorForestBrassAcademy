## Notes

The Hugo Burge Foundation's Creative Education Fund is specifically for projects supporting young people (0–29) to build creative knowledge and skills. This maps directly to WFBA's core programmes:
- Outreach workshops: first access for school-age children
- Year 4 school brass teaching: whole year-group access to brass instruments
- Starter band: progression from first access

**No charity registration required** — this is a rare and valuable characteristic. WFBA can apply now without a charity number.

**Co-funding note:** For applications over £10,000, 30% co-funding is required from other sources. WFBA could potentially use:
- In-kind contributions (volunteer tutor time, Royal Holloway venue, instrument stock)
- A concurrent Awards for All award as a confirmed funding partner
- Member subscriptions or self-generated income

An application in the £8,000–£12,000 range is likely most realistic for a first application from a new organisation without a track record.

**Access support fund:** £7,000 available at up to £300 per access worker for disabled/neurodivergent applicants — likely not relevant but worth noting.

## Eligibility evidence

Grants up to £15,000. No charity registration requirement stated. UK-wide. Eligible applicants include "schools, local authorities, community groups, charities, museum/gallery spaces, educational institutions and arts organisations." Opens June 1, 2026 via Submittable, closes July 31, 2026. Source: [hugoburgefoundation.org/grants](https://www.hugoburgefoundation.org/grants), checked 2026-05-09.
