## Notes

The Radcliffe Trust funds classical music in the UK, with emphasis on chamber music, composition, and music education. Special interests include music for children and adults with special needs, youth orchestras, and secondary/higher education projects. Grants are £2,500–£5,000.

**The December 2026 consideration round is currently open (May 1 – July 31, 2026).** If WFBA is eligible, there is time to prepare and submit an application by 31 July 2026. Projects would start from 1 January 2027.

**Programme fit question:** WFBA's outreach work involves teaching children and young people brass instruments — this could fall under "music education" and "youth orchestras/ensemble work." However, brass bands are historically associated with band/brass culture rather than classical chamber music. The Trust's specific mention of "chamber music, composition" suggests a leaning toward traditional classical genres. Brass can be classical (brass quintets, orchestral brass) but a community brass band's framing may feel different to the Trust. Direct confirmation is essential.

**Legal form question:** The Trust accepts "CICs, CIOs, and other not-for-profit organisations" — this is broader than registered charities only. However, it is not clear whether a constituted but unregistered community organisation without any formal registration qualifies. Confirmation needed.

**Best application frame if eligible:** Year 4 school brass programme as a music education project for young people — framed around first access to a classical instrument (brass is a standard orchestral/classical instrument family), learning to play in ensemble, and building musical skills. This is more likely to resonate than framing as community brass band development.
