## Notes

Reaching Communities is one of the largest accessible grant funds in England. It funds up to £500k over 5 years, with most grants under £500k. The application is substantial (evidence of community need, theory of change, organisational track record) but the missions map well:

- **"Help children and young people thrive"** — developing positive social and emotional skills through music access.
- **"Help communities come together"** — inclusive spaces and activities for all ages; community music participation.

No charity registration barrier — constituted voluntary organisations are explicitly eligible.

**Why monitor rather than apply now:** The minimum grant of £20,001 is achievable, but Reaching Communities expects strong evidence of the community problem being addressed, the organisation's track record, and a credible multi-year delivery plan. WFBA is pre-registration, pre-outreach-delivery, and building its evidence base. An application now would be weak. The best use of this opportunity is to build toward it — let outreach delivery run for 6–12 months, collect participant data, then apply.

**Realistic timeline:** Application feasible from Q1 2027, funded period could be 2027–2029/30.

**Key requirements to build toward:**
- Evidence of community need (school deprivation data, geography rationale)
- Participant numbers and feedback from initial outreach
- Organisational governance (trustees, constitution, financials)
- Multi-year delivery plan with budget
- Partner endorsement letters (schools, Royal Holloway)
