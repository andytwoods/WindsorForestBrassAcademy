## Notes

**This is one of the strongest new opportunities found.** The Round 3 theme ("children and young people's instrumental music education, choirs excluded") maps almost exactly onto WFBA's core mission. Brass education is instrumental — not choral — so the choir exclusion is not a barrier.

**Best programme to pitch:** Year 4 school brass teaching (whole-class access, first instrument experience, progression pathway) or outreach workshops (reaching multiple schools and age groups). Both frame brass as access activity for children who would not otherwise participate.

**Eligibility for unregistered organisations:** The guidelines state the trust "will consider applications from social enterprises and community groups where they are formally constituted and have clear charitable purposes." WFBA has a constitution and charitable aims. This should qualify, but confirm directly before applying.

**Grant size:** Typical awards are from a few hundred pounds to below £10,000 for most grants. Multi-year grants up to £15,000/year in exceptional cases. A £3,000–£8,000 ask for a defined outreach or school programme is realistic.

**Application process:** Two-stage — Stage 1 is a short application form. If shortlisted, organisations are invited to submit a Stage 2 application. All applications submitted via online Grants System. 5pm deadline is firm, no exceptions.

**Exclusions check:**
- Schools cannot apply — WFBA applies, not the school — pass
- No capital projects — instrument purchase as capital would need careful framing; outreach delivery costs are programme expenditure — pass for delivery costs
- No retrospective work — apply for future programme — pass
- No individuals — WFBA is an organisation — pass

**Timing:** September window (1–15 Sept) is short. Prepare draft in August. If WFBA is mid-delivery of school outreach by September 2026, use early evidence from September 2026 as context rather than funding for ongoing activity.
